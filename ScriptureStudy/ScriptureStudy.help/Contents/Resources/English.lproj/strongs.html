Strong's Numbers

Look up the original Hebrew or Greek word behind any English word in the text.

What are Strong's numbers?

Strong's Exhaustive Concordance assigned a unique number to every distinct word in the Hebrew Old Testament and Greek New Testament. When your Bible module includes Strong's tagging, each word in the text is linked to its original language entry — allowing you to look up the precise meaning, grammar and usage of any word without knowing Hebrew or Greek.

Installing a Strong's lexicon

You need two things: a Bible module with Strong's tagging, and a Strong's dictionary module. Many free options are available in the MyBible community. Once both are in your modules folder, ScriptureStudy detects them automatically — Strong's modules are identified by the is_strong = true flag in their info table and shown with a purple H□ icon in the Library.

Selecting your lexicon

In the Companion panel, switch to the Dictionaries tab. The Strong's picker at the top of that section lets you choose which lexicon to use. Your selection is saved automatically.

Looking up a word

When a Strong's-tagged Bible is loaded, the words in each verse appear as interactive tokens. Tap any word to look up its entry in the Companion panel's Dictionaries tab. The definition appears immediately, showing the original word, transliteration, pronunciation and meaning.

OT and NT detection

ScriptureStudy automatically selects the correct part of the lexicon based on the book you are reading — Hebrew entries (H numbers) for Old Testament books, Greek entries (G numbers) for New Testament books. This happens automatically based on the book number.

The Strong's flash

The first time you load a Strong's-tagged Bible in a session, the tagged words briefly flash to let you know they are interactive. This behaviour can be turned off in Settings → Behaviour → Flash Strong's words on load.
