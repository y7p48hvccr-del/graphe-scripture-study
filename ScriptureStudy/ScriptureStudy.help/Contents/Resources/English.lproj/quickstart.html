Quick Start

Get up and reading in under five minutes.

Step 1 — Choose a modules folder

Open the Library tab (the books icon in the tab bar). Click Choose Modules Folder and select the folder on your Mac where you keep or plan to keep your MyBible .SQLite3 files. ScriptureStudy will scan this folder automatically every time you launch.

A good location is ~/Documents/MyBible Modules/ — create it if it does not exist yet.

Step 2 — Add a Bible

Download a free MyBible Bible module from the internet (search for "MyBible SQLite3 modules"). Place the .SQLite3 file in your modules folder. ScriptureStudy will detect it on the next scan — click the refresh button in the Library toolbar to scan immediately.

In the Library, your Bible will appear under Bibles. Click Use to make it your active translation.

Step 3 — Start reading

Switch to the Bible tab. Use the Book and Chapter menus in the left sidebar to navigate. Your selected Bible text appears in the main reading area.

Step 4 — Open the Companion panel

The Companion panel opens to the right of the Bible text. Use the tab bar at the top of the Companion panel to switch between Comparison, Commentary, Dictionaries, Notes and Web views.

Next steps: Install a Strong's lexicon to unlock word-by-word Hebrew and Greek definitions — see Strong's Numbers. Install a commentary to read alongside the text — see Commentary.
