Organizer

Calendar, reading plans, portraits and personal notes

Layout

The Organizer tab is divided into two panels. The left panel contains the calendar at the top and a portrait photo grid below. The right panel shows the reading plan card, a daily summary of prayer and reminder items, and the note editor at the bottom.

Calendar

The calendar occupies the top portion of the left panel. It shows the current month with Monday as the first day of the week. The header bar shows the month and year alongside the phrase I'm organised! Navigation arrows move between months and a Today button returns to the current date.

Each day cell can show coloured indicator dots:

Dot colourMeaning
BlueA reading plan passage is scheduled for this day
GreenThis day has been marked as complete

Tap a day to select it and update the right panel. Long-press a day to open the event popover.

Prayer and reminder popover

Long-pressing a calendar day opens a popover showing all prayer requests and reminders for that day. From here you can view, tick off, delete, or add items, and set a repeat rule — Once, Weekly, or Daily.

Portrait panel

The lower portion of the left panel contains four portrait slots arranged in a 2×2 grid with rounded corners and a bold black border. These slots are for photos of family members, friends, prayer partners or your pastor — people you want to keep in mind as you study and pray.

  • Adding a photo — click an empty slot to open a file picker, or drag an image from Finder directly onto a slot
  • Removing a photo — right-click a filled slot and choose Remove Photo
  • Accepted formats — JPEG, PNG, HEIC, TIFF

Photos are saved privately to your Mac and persist between launches.

Reading plan card

The reading plan card shows the Bible passage for the selected day. A picker lets you switch between installed reading plan modules. Tap Read → to navigate the Bible panel to that passage.

Day summary

Below the reading plan card, a summary shows all prayer requests and reminders for the selected day. Tick buttons mark items as done. The + button opens the event popover.

Marking a day complete

The Mark Complete button in the top-right of the right panel marks the selected day as done. The day's calendar cell turns green. Tap the button again to un-mark it.

Note editor

The bottom of the right panel is a full word processor showing your most recently created verse note. When you long-press a verse number in the Bible panel, the new note appears here automatically. Notes are saved automatically — there is no save button.
