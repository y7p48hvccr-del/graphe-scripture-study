Quick Start

Get reading in three steps

Step 1 — Choose your modules folder

ScriptureStudy reads MyBible-format SQLite files from a folder on your Mac or iCloud Drive. Go to the Library tab and click Choose Modules Folder. Point it to wherever you keep your .SQLite3 module files. The app scans the folder and detects all installed modules automatically.

Your modules folder can be anywhere — a local folder, iCloud Drive, or a Dropbox/OneDrive folder. The app just needs read access.

Step 2 — Select a Bible

Once modules are loaded, go to the Library tab and find your Bible in the Bibles section. Click Use to make it your active Bible. Then click the Bible tab to start reading.

Step 3 — Start reading

Use the picker bar at the top of the Bible panel to choose your Bible version, book and chapter. Tap any verse number to select it — this loads cross-references in the companion panel automatically.

What to do next

  • Long-press a verse number to create a note anchored to that verse
  • Tap any word to look up its Strong's number (if your Bible has Strong's tagging)
  • Open the companion panel to see cross-references, commentary or a second Bible version alongside your main reading
  • Visit the Organizer tab to set up a reading plan and daily prayer items
  • Visit the Devotional tab to read your daily devotional
