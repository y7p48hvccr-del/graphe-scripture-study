Pricing Overview

Graphē One Codex

Core, Reference, and Professional

Choose the level of Codex that fits your reading practice. Codex is designed as a high-trust reading environment for complex texts. Begin with a serious core reader, move into deeper reference and annotation tools when needed, and scale into advanced professional workflows over time.

Codex Core

A focused reading environment for EPUBs, PDFs, and structured texts.

  • Persistent local-first library
  • Built-in reference texts included
  • EPUB and PDF import
  • Bookmarks and reading position
  • Structured navigation and contents
  • Offline reading
  • Privacy-first foundations

Codex Core is intended to feel complete: a serious everyday reader for long-form and structured works.

Codex Reference

Deeper reference, annotation, and study tools for readers working across complex texts.

  • Everything in Codex Core
  • Richer notes and highlights
  • Cross references and linked passages
  • Deeper navigation across large structured works
  • Reference overlays and contextual aids
  • Advanced bookmarking and reading trails
  • Text comparison tools
  • Expanded study and reference workflows

Especially useful for readers working with reference texts, legal material, technical documents, long-form scholarly works, richly structured documents, and exegesis.

Codex Professional

Advanced research and professional reading workflows for large, demanding document collections.

  • Everything in lower tiers
  • Advanced annotation system
  • Notes, tags, notebooks, and research collections
  • Cross-document linking
  • Parallel and comparative reading
  • Advanced search across the library
  • Citation, excerpt, and export tools
  • Structured navigation for very large works
  • Richer sync for workspaces and metadata
  • Professional workflows for legal, academic, technical, governmental, and institutional reading

Codex Professional is in active development. Register interest to receive updates on progress, availability, and professional or institutional access.

Register Interest

At a Glance

Core for serious reading. Reference for deeper study, annotation, and linked reading. Professional for intensive research and large-document work.

Incremental Release

Codex may be introduced incrementally. The initial release may begin with Codex Core and Codex Reference, with Codex Professional developing later as a broader research and institutional layer.

Approach

Codex Core should feel complete. Codex Reference should deepen study and reference work without narrowing the app's identity. Codex Professional should extend the same reading environment into a full research workspace for large, structured collections.
